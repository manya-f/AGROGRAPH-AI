# 🤖 AI-Assisted Early Detection: Simulation Findings & Recommendations

> *This report synthesises results from the AI Impact Simulation modelling three early-detection improvement scenarios (20%, 30%, 40% efficiency gain) against a 2022 baseline of **2,447 kt** total pesticide active ingredient and **$119.09/ha** average input cost.*

---

## 1. 🌿 Total Pesticide Reduction Potential

AI-driven early detection enables precision pest intervention — catching outbreaks before economic thresholds are crossed, eliminating prophylactic sprays and reducing over-application. The simulation projects:

| AI Detection Efficiency | Total Usage (kt a.i.) | Reduction vs. Baseline | Usage Saved (kt) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Traditional (0%)** | 2,446.7 | — | — |
| **AI-Assisted 20%** | 2,126.3 | **−13.1%** | 320.4 kt |
| **AI-Assisted 30%** | 1,966.2 | **−19.6%** | 480.5 kt |
| **AI-Assisted 40%** | 1,805.9 | **−26.2%** | 640.7 kt |

At best-case (40% efficiency), AI could eliminate **640 kt** of pesticide use annually — equivalent to removing the entire annual output of most mid-sized agricultural nations. Even a conservative 20% detection uplift cuts usage by **13%**, a meaningful first step.

## 6. ✅ Actionable Recommendations

### 🎯 For Farmers

**1. Prioritise AI adoption for high-value, high-loss crops first.**
Cotton, vegetables, and rice offer the fastest ROI on AI pest-detection investment due to their extreme baseline vulnerability (95–98% potential loss). Deploying drone-based scouting or IoT sensor networks on these crops can recoup technology costs within 1–2 seasons.

**2. Target insecticide reduction as the primary AI use case.**
Insecticides are the highest-cost, highest-risk, fastest-savings category. AI early-detection tools — whether smartphone-based image recognition, pheromone traps with data connectivity, or aerial NDVI analysis — directly attack the economic threshold problem that drives over-application. A 30–40% cut in insecticide use is achievable with current commercially available technology.

### 🏛️ For Policymakers

**3. Implement AI adoption subsidies tied to measurable pesticide reduction.**
The simulation shows even a 20% detection efficiency gain saves $17/ha — sufficient to justify public co-investment in precision agriculture tools for smallholders. Subsidy schemes should tie payments to verified reduction in applied active ingredient (kg/ha), using national pesticide registry data, rather than rewarding adoption of specific technologies.

**4. Prioritise AI-assisted fungicide management to address the fastest-growing cost category.**
Fungicide costs grew +126% from 2000–2022 — the steepest increase of any category — and resistance is an accelerating risk. AI disease forecasting models (based on weather, humidity, and historical outbreak data) can reduce prophylactic fungicide applications by up to 34%, cutting costs while slowing resistance development. Governments should fund disease-forecasting platforms as shared agricultural infrastructure.
