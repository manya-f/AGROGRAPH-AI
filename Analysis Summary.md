# 🌱 Pesticide & AI Impact Analysis — Key Findings Summary

> *Based on a 2022 global baseline of **2,447 kt** total pesticide active ingredient and **$119.09/ha** average input cost, across three AI early-detection scenarios (20%, 30%, and 40% efficiency gain).*

---

## 1. 🌿 Pesticide Reduction

AI-assisted early detection consistently reduces pesticide use across all scenarios, with a **13–26% reduction range** depending on how well the AI performs.

| Scenario | Total Usage (kt) | Saved vs. Baseline | Reduction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Traditional (no AI) | 2,446.7 kt | — | — |
| AI 20% efficiency | 2,126.3 kt | 320 kt | −13.1% |
| AI 30% efficiency | 1,966.2 kt | 481 kt | −19.6% |
| **AI 40% efficiency** | **1,806.0 kt** | **641 kt** | **−26.2%** |

**Headline figure: 640 kt saved** at 40% AI efficiency — roughly equivalent to the entire annual pesticide output of a mid-sized agricultural nation, eliminated through smarter, earlier intervention.

---

## 2. 💰 Cost Savings

Farmers spend less on pesticides when AI catches problems early — because they only spray when and where it's actually needed.

### Per-Hectare Savings

| Scenario | Cost / ha | Saving / ha | % Reduction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Traditional | $119.09 | — | — |
| AI 20% | $101.91 | **$17.18** | 14.4% |
| AI 30% | $93.32 | **$25.77** | 21.6% |
| **AI 40%** | **$84.72** | **$34.37** | **28.9%** |

For a 1,000 ha farm at 40% AI efficiency, that's **~$34,370 saved per year** — money that can be reinvested in soil health, equipment, or technology.

### Global Scale Estimate

Applying these per-hectare savings across ~470 million ha of global cropland under typical pesticide use:

| Scenario | Global Savings (Est.) |
|---|---|
| AI 20% efficiency | ~$8.1 billion / year |
| AI 30% efficiency | ~$12.1 billion / year |
| **AI 40% efficiency** | **~$16.2 billion / year** |

> These are direct input cost savings only — before accounting for reduced healthcare costs, environmental clean-up, or pesticide resistance management.

## 3. 🌍 Environmental Benefits

Not all pesticides are equal in terms of harm. Insecticides score highest on environmental impact (EIQ score: 78/100) and are the most dangerous to bees (toxicity: 9/10) — and they're exactly where AI delivers the **largest proportional reduction**.

### Environmental Impact by Category (Baseline)

| Category | EIQ Score | Bee Toxicity | Usage Share | AI 40% Reduction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Insecticides** | 78 / 100 ⚠️ | 9 / 10 🐝 | 20% | **−40%** ⭐ |
| Herbicides | 42 / 100 | 3 / 10 | 55% | −20% |
| Fungicides | 35 / 100 | 2 / 10 | 13% | −34% |
| Other | 28 / 100 | 2 / 10 | 12% | −24% |

**Key takeaways:**
- **Insecticides** see the biggest cut (−40%) and are the most ecologically harmful — AI delivers maximum environmental dividend precisely where it matters most.
- **Fungicides** (cost grew +126% from 2000–2022) benefit significantly from AI disease forecasting (−34%), slowing both costs and resistance development.
- The aggregate **EIQ improvement** is amplified because AI disproportionately reduces the highest-harm categories, not just total volume.

## 💡 How Does AI Early Detection Actually Work?

*In plain language — no jargon.*

Think of a traditional farmer like someone who checks their crops every week or two, and sprays pesticides "just in case" — or only after they already see widespread damage. By then, the problem has often spread, and a full field-wide spray feels necessary.

**AI early detection changes the timing.** Sensors, cameras, or drones monitor crops continuously. An AI model analyses the images and data to spot the very first signs of pest activity — tiny leaf damage, early fungal spots, or the first few insects — days or weeks before a human eye would notice.

Because the farmer gets an alert early, they can:
1. **Spray only the affected area**, not the whole field
2. **Use less pesticide** because the problem is still small
3. **Act before resistance develops** because they're not doing blanket preventative sprays

It's the difference between treating a small infection with a targeted antibiotic vs. pre-emptively taking full-body radiation "just in case." The AI makes farming *reactive to real evidence* rather than *preventative by habit* — and that's where the savings and environmental gains come from.
